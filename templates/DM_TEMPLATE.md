# Direct Outreach Templates — Founder DMs, Cold Emails, Informal Arrangements

*Use these templates when the best path to an opportunity is not an ATS portal but a direct
conversation with a founder, CTO, or hiring manager. Many hard tech startups under 200 people
hire through relationships, not job boards.*

> **When to use DM vs. cold email vs. ATS:**
> - Founder is active on X/Twitter and responds to DMs → DM first
> - Founder is not on X but company has < 200 people → cold email to CEO/CTO
> - Company has > 200 people but no materials intern posting → cold email to VP Engineering or R&D lead
> - Company has a formal ATS posting → apply through ATS, then follow up with DM/email
> - You've already applied and want to stand out → DM the hiring manager

---

## Template 1: X/Twitter DM — Initial Contact (Under 280 Characters)

*The first DM must fit in one tweet-length message. You have one shot to not look like spam.
Name something specific. Be direct about what you want.*

---

### Option A: Technical Hook

> [THEIR HANDLE] — your [SPECIFIC PRODUCT/PAPER/ANNOUNCEMENT] is directly connected to my
> PhD work on [YOUR TOPIC]. Materials science PhD at [UNIVERSITY], looking for summer
> manufacturing experience. Worth a quick conversation?

*Character count target: 250-280.*

### Option B: Factory/Scale-Up Hook

> [THEIR HANDLE] — saw you're scaling [SPECIFIC FACTORY/LINE]. I study how materials fail
> at interfaces — the exact problem that shows up at production scale. PhD at [UNIVERSITY],
> active clearance. Can I send a 1-pager?

*Character count target: 240-270.*

### Option C: Mutual Context Hook

> [THEIR HANDLE] — [MUTUAL CONNECTION/CONTEXT] pointed me your way. PhD materials scientist
> at [UNIVERSITY], [CLEARANCE STATUS]. Working on [1-LINE THESIS]. Looking for summer floor
> time. Open to a quick chat?

*Character count target: 230-260.*

## Template 2: Follow-Up DM (If They Respond Positively)

## Template 3: Cold Email to Founder/CTO (Under 200 Words)

*For when the founder isn't on X or doesn't respond to DMs. Same principles: specific hook,
brief thesis, direct ask.*

---

**Subject:** PhD intern — [your specific method] + [their specific product/process]

---

[THEIR FIRST NAME],

[LINE 1 — SPECIFIC HOOK. Name something they built, announced, or published. Not "I admire
your company." Something like: "Your $X raise to scale [specific product] caught my attention
— the [specific materials challenge] you're solving is directly connected to my PhD work."]

I'm a [YEAR] PhD at [UNIVERSITY] working on [SPECIFIC THESIS — one sentence with a number].
I've worked at [PRIOR EMPLOYER] on [RELEVANT WORK — one sentence]. I hold [CLEARANCE STATUS
if relevant].

formal program — I'm interested in contributing to [SPECIFIC TEAM/PROCESS] and I'll take
whatever arrangement makes sense for your team.

Would a 15-minute call be possible? Happy to send a 1-page technical summary first.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL] | [YOUR LINKEDIN]
PhD Candidate, [FIELD], [UNIVERSITY]

---

*Total: 150-190 words. Do not include attachments.*

## Template 4: Informal Arrangement Pitch

*For situations where there's no formal intern program and you're proposing something
non-standard. This works best at companies under 100 people where the founder makes
hiring decisions directly.*

---

**Subject:** Summer materials scientist — informal arrangement

---

[THEIR FIRST NAME],

I'll be direct: I'm a materials science PhD at [UNIVERSITY] and I want to spend 10-12
weeks this summer on your manufacturing floor. Not as a formal intern — as whatever
arrangement works for you.

What I bring:
- [DIFFERENTIATOR 1 — e.g., "Active Secret clearance"]
- [DIFFERENTIATOR 2 — e.g., "Computational materials pipeline (LAMMPS/VASP/Python)

What I'm looking for:

  team needs capacity on

I'm flexible on compensation and structure. The experience matters more than the title.

Worth a 15-minute call?

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL]

## Template 5: X/Twitter Engagement Before DM

*Before DMing a founder, engage with their content for 1-2 weeks. This makes your DM
not-cold. Here's how:*

### Week 1-2: Engagement Sequence

1. **Follow them** and turn on notifications for their posts
2. **Reply to 2-3 of their technical posts** with substantive comments (not "great post!"):
   - Add a data point they didn't mention
   - Ask a specific technical question
   - Share a relevant paper or finding
3. **Quote-tweet one post** with your own technical take (tag your research area)
4. **Then DM** — reference a specific post you engaged with

### Example Engagement Reply:

> Interesting — the [thermal/mechanical/processing] challenge you're describing maps to
> what I see in simulation. At [SPECIFIC CONDITION], the interface fails at [SPECIFIC
> MECHANISM]. Happy to share the data if useful.

*This establishes you as a technical peer, not a job-seeker. The DM lands differently
after 2-3 substantive replies.*

## Do NOT:

- DM more than one person at the same company on the same day
- Send identical DMs to multiple founders (they talk to each other)
- Lead with "I'm looking for an internship" — lead with the technical connection
- DM someone at 2 AM their time zone
- Follow up more than once on a DM (if they don't respond, they're not interested)
- Use ChatGPT-sounding language ("I'm passionate about...", "I'd love the opportunity to...")
- Mention this repo or that you used AI to draft the message

---

*These templates produce first drafts. Customize every message with specific details about
the founder's work, their company's current stage, and your genuine technical connection.
A customized 250-character DM beats a perfect 2000-word email every time.*
