# Cold Outreach Template

*Use this template when a company has no active ATS posting but is a strong fit, when you want
to reach a hiring manager directly before or alongside a formal application, or when following
up after submission with no response.*

> **When to use outreach vs. ATS application:**
> - Company has no posting but is a strong fit → outreach only
> - Company has a posting that is clearly understaffed or unusual → ATS application + outreach simultaneously
> - Applied via ATS 2+ weeks ago, no response → follow-up outreach
> - Referral available through a mutual connection → outreach before ATS application

---

## Step 1: Research Before You Write

Do not send a cold email without completing this checklist. An email that shows no research is
deleted in 3 seconds. An email that names something specific takes 30 seconds to read — and gets
responses.

**Find:**

- [ ] The correct person to contact. Priority order:
  1. Hiring manager for the team (not the recruiter unless you have no other option)
  2. A senior engineer or scientist doing work similar to what you want to do
  3. A company recruiter or HR contact as a last resort

  *Where to find: LinkedIn (search "[Company] [team keyword] intern mentor" or "[Company] staff
  engineer materials"), the company's "about" or "team" page, academic papers with company
  affiliation (corresponding author is a good target), GitHub org members.*

- [ ] Their name, correct spelling, and LinkedIn or email if available.

- [ ] One specific technical thing they published, presented, shipped, or worked on that
  connects to your background. This is the most important piece of research. It becomes
  line 1 of your email.
  *Where to find: Google Scholar, company blog, LinkedIn activity, conference proceedings,
  patent database (USPTO, Google Patents), GitHub.*

- [ ] Whether the company has any open internship postings at all (even if not in your exact
  area). This tells you whether they have an internship program active this cycle.

- [ ] Any recent company news (funding round, new contract, product launch, partnership) that
  you can reference authentically. Do not manufacture interest — only cite news that
  genuinely connects to your work.

**Email address format:**
If you don't have a direct email, try common patterns:
- `firstname.lastname@company.com`
- `flastname@company.com`
- `firstnamelastname@company.com`
- Verify with Hunter.io or LinkedIn Sales Navigator if available.
- Worst case: use LinkedIn InMail or the company contact form.

## Step 2: Cold Email Script

*Target length: under 200 words. Every sentence earns its place.*

---

**Subject Line Options** (choose one — do not use all three):

1. `PhD intern inquiry — [your specific tool/method] + [their specific application area]`
   *e.g.: "PhD intern inquiry — LAMMPS/VASP + high-entropy alloy qualification"*

2. `Research connection — [your topic] and [their specific published work or program]`
   *e.g.: "Research connection — grain boundary segregation and your 2024 NiCoCr study"*

3. `[Mutual connection name] suggested I reach out — PhD intern inquiry`
   *Only use this one if a mutual connection actually told you to reach out.*

---

**Email Body:**

---

[THEIR FIRST NAME],

[LINE 1 — SPECIFIC HOOK — required. Name the specific paper, product, program, or result that
connects your work to theirs. This line makes you real. Do NOT start with "I am a PhD student
at..." or "I found your company on LinkedIn."

Example: "Your 2024 Materials Today paper on dislocation-mediated fatigue in NiCoCr alloys
directly overlaps with my dissertation work — I've been using a similar MD/EAM approach but
applied to fracture toughness prediction in AM microstructures."]

[LINE 2-3 — YOUR THESIS, BRIEFLY — 1-2 sentences. What do you do, and why does it matter to
them? Pull from POSITIONING_TEMPLATE.md Section 7. Keep it concrete. Name the tool, the scale,
the result.]

[LINE 4-5 — THE ASK — what specifically are you asking for? A conversation, a referral, an
informational interview, information about an internship program. Be direct. Do not ask "do you
have any openings?" — that reads as form letter. Ask for something specific and small.

Example: "I would value a 20-minute conversation about your team's simulation approach and
whether there's a fit for a PhD intern contribution this summer. I'm happy to share a brief
technical summary of my current work beforehand if that's useful."]

[YOUR FIRST NAME]
[YOUR EMAIL] | [YOUR LINKEDIN URL]
PhD Candidate, [FIELD], [UNIVERSITY], expected [MONTH YEAR]

---

*Total word count target: 120-180 words.*

**Do not include:**
- Attachment in the first email (send resume only if they ask)
- A list of your skills or courses
- The phrase "I am passionate about"
- The phrase "I look forward to hearing from you" (too passive — close with a question instead)
- More than one hyperlink

## Step 3: LinkedIn Note

*Use when sending a connection request on LinkedIn, or when InMail is the only option.*
*Hard limit: 300 characters (LinkedIn connection note cap).*

---

Template:

> [THEIR FIRST NAME], your team's work on [SPECIFIC TOPIC] connects directly to my dissertation
> on [YOUR TOPIC]. I'm a PhD candidate at [UNIVERSITY] exploring [1-2 word descriptor] internship
> opportunities. Happy to share a brief technical summary.

*Character count check: Draft the note in a character counter before sending. LinkedIn cuts off
at 300 with no warning.*

---

## Step 4: Follow-Up Timing and Protocol

*One follow-up is professional. Two follow-ups is the limit. Three is spam.*

| Trigger | When to Send | What to Send |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| No reply to cold email | 10-14 days after initial | Brief follow-up (below) |
| No reply to follow-up | 3 weeks after follow-up | Single final note (below) |
| Applied via ATS, no response | 14 days after submission | Brief check-in to recruiter |
| Rejection received | N/A | Optional: brief thank-you and ask to be considered for future cycles |

**First Follow-Up (10-14 days after initial email):**

---

Subject: Re: [original subject line]

[THEIR FIRST NAME],

Following up on my note from [DATE]. I wanted to make sure it didn't get buried — happy to share
a brief summary of my current research if that would help evaluate fit.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]

---

*Total: ~40 words. Do not re-explain your background. Just re-surface the thread.*

**Final Follow-Up (if still no response after first follow-up):**

---

Subject: Re: [original subject line]

[THEIR FIRST NAME],

Last note — I don't want to clutter your inbox. If there's no capacity for a PhD intern this
cycle, I completely understand. If circumstances change, I'd still welcome a conversation.

[YOUR FIRST NAME]

---

*This note closes the loop professionally and leaves a positive impression even with no response.*

## Tracking

After sending each outreach, log it in your tracker:

| Date Sent | Company | Contact Name | Contact Email/LinkedIn | Type (cold/follow-up) | Response? | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|-----------|-------|
| [DATE] | [COMPANY] | [NAME] | [EMAIL OR LI URL] | [cold] | [No / Yes — date] | [NOTES] |

*Outreach tracking prevents duplicate contact and helps you identify which subject lines and
hooks generate responses. Review the tracker after 20 outreach messages and adjust your approach.*
