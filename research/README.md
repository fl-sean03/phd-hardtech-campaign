## Scoring System

All entries include a fit score (0–100) reflecting the alignment between a PhD materials science profile and the role. Scores consider:

- Technical overlap with the role's day-to-day work
- Whether clearance or national lab background conveys value
- Manufacturing floor vs. research depth (manufacturing floor experience weighted higher for summer 2026 targeting)
- Engagement accessibility (can you reach the decision-maker directly?)
- Geographic considerations noted but not scored

Scores above 80 are priority applications. Scores 70–79 are strong secondaries. Scores below 70 are situational or speculative.

## Key Differentiators to Reference in Applications

The following candidate attributes appear repeatedly across the research as differentiating factors. Substitute your own equivalents where placeholders appear in the files:

- **[Your active security clearance]**: Secret or higher. A hard filter at defense SRM sites (Raytheon Tucson, Northrop Roy UT, L3Harris Culpeper). Most applicants lack one.
- **[Your prior national lab or DoD lab experience]**: Familiarity with DoD-funded research environments, processing window methodology, and reproducible documentation practice.
- **[Your prior DOE program or I-Corps experience]**: A cultural signal at ARPA-E portfolio companies (Antora, ION Storage, Noon Energy) and critical minerals companies (Redwood, Ascend Elements). These companies are run by people who went through DOE commercialization programs.
- **[Your computational stack]**: LAMMPS, VASP, Quantum ESPRESSO, Python. Directly applicable across semiconductor, quantum, science AI, and battery materials roles.
- **[Your institution]**: Substitute your university name where it appears.
- **[Your advisor]**: Substitute for advisor-specific references (e.g., NSF INTERN advisor conversations).
