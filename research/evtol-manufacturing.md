# eVTOL Manufacturing — Research

*Research current as of March 2026. Verify URLs and deadlines before applying.*

*Focus: eVTOL aircraft manufacturing, carbon-fiber composite scale production, battery integration, thermal management, and FAA certification-driven materials qualification. Note: active security clearance is NOT a differentiator in commercial aviation — these companies are not defense-classified. Lead with composites manufacturing depth, computational materials background, and certification-readiness framing instead.*

## Joby Aviation — Composite Manufacturing Engineering Intern, Summer 2026
- **Score estimate:** 72/100
- **Sector:** Advanced Air Mobility — eVTOL Carbon-Fiber Composites Manufacturing
- **Location:** Marina, CA (onsite, Monterey Bay area manufacturing facility)
- **Timing:** Summer 2026 (active posting; Joby is in final-phase FAA certification and actively scaling production)
- **Apply URL:** https://careers-jobyaviation.icims.com/ (search "Composite Manufacturing Internship"); also listed on LinkedIn and EV.Careers
- **Pay:** $22.00–$31.00/hr (undergraduate), $5,550–$7,800/month (graduate student)

**Fit analysis:** Joby is the furthest along of any eVTOL company toward FAA type certification. As of early 2026, they have begun power-on testing of their first FAA-conforming aircraft and are entering the Type Inspection Authorization (TIA) flight testing phase, with FAA pilots scheduled to conduct their own tests in 2026. The Marina, CA facility is the pilot production line where composite layup and aircraft assembly happen at rate. Joby also operates a scaled production facility in Dayton, Ohio, and in January 2026 acquired a second Ohio facility (700,000+ sq ft) for $61.5M to double production capacity to four aircraft per month. The Composite Manufacturing Engineering Intern role involves creating and updating SOPs and work instructions for composite layup and assembly, providing technical support to production floor personnel, and supporting lean manufacturing initiatives including cycle time data collection and scrap/rework reduction. For a PhD materials science student, the fit is in the documentation-to-floor translation layer: understanding why composite parts fail or require rework, and contributing to root-cause investigation for non-conforming parts. The computational background is useful for process optimization and data-driven manufacturing analysis, though the role is primarily hands-on floor work, not simulation. Joby's certification-driven quality requirements (FAA Part 23/21.17(b) special conditions) mean that every manufacturing process change requires formal materials qualification — this is directly analogous to the kind of process-structure-property documentation work that connects to materials science PhD training.

**Potential gaps:** Joby is not defense, so clearance and prior national lab work do not convey the same signal they would at RTX or Northrop. Many composites-focused undergrads with layup experience may be preferred over a PhD candidate whose hands-on composites time is limited. The role description reads as manufacturing engineering support, not materials R&D — a PhD candidate needs to frame their value as bringing deeper analytical capability to the production floor, not as being overqualified for the work. Marina, CA is a real relocation (Monterey Bay area, moderate cost of living). Graduate student pay at $5,550–$7,800/month is competitive for an internship.

## Archer Aviation — M&P Engineering Intern, Summer 2026
- **Score estimate:** 70/100
- **Sector:** Advanced Air Mobility — eVTOL Composites, Battery Systems, Materials & Processes
- **Location:** San Jose, CA (HQ, onsite); manufacturing facility in Covington, GA
- **Timing:** Summer 2026, 10-week program (May 26–Jul 31 or Jun 15–Aug 21, 2026)
- **Apply URL:** https://job-boards.greenhouse.io/archer56 (search M&P Engineering Intern or Manufacturing Intern); also https://www.archer.com/Careers
- **Pay:** ~$25.00–$35.00/hr (based on BMS Test Engineer Intern posting; average intern pay ~$29.59/hr per Glassdoor)

**Fit analysis:** Archer is building the Midnight eVTOL aircraft, backed by United Airlines and Stellantis, with a completed 400,000 sq ft high-volume manufacturing facility in Covington, GA. The M&P (Materials & Processes) Engineering Intern role is the strongest materials-science fit of their intern tracks. The position involves supporting material qualification programs, executing mechanical testing on CFRP (carbon fiber reinforced polymer), adhesives, metals, and bonded joints, collaborating with design/manufacturing/certification teams, and applying materials and processes specifications to FAA regulatory standards. For a PhD materials scientist, the hook is direct: this is certification-driven materials qualification work. You would be conducting coupon-level and process-level testing that feeds into the data package Archer needs for FAA type certification. The computational background supports data analysis and test matrix design, while prior national lab experience (materials characterization, failure analysis, systematic documentation) maps cleanly onto the M&P engineering workflow. Archer is also ramping battery pack design and BMS (Battery Management System) testing — candidates with any thermal management or electrochemistry exposure could angle toward the BMS Test Engineer Intern track as a secondary option. The Covington, GA factory is targeting Stellantis-style automotive production scaling (650 aircraft/year by 2030), which means manufacturing process repeatability and materials consistency are existential concerns for the company.

**Potential gaps:** The M&P Engineering Intern posting is based in San Jose (HQ), not at the Covington manufacturing facility — relocation and housing are not provided. A PhD candidate with no prior hands-on composites testing experience (wet saws, mechanical test frames, adhesive bonding) will need to emphasize transferable characterization skills from national lab work. Archer's intern program is structured as a 10-week cohort experience, which may feel constrained for a PhD student accustomed to open-ended research timelines. Competition from MS-level materials engineering students with direct composites coursework will be real. Confirm that the M&P Engineering Intern posting is still active for Summer 2026 on Greenhouse — the listing found was on third-party boards and may have been from a prior cycle.

## Beta Technologies — 2026-2027 BETA Internship (Vermont)
- **Score estimate:** 67/100
- **Sector:** Advanced Air Mobility — Electric Aircraft (eCTOL/eVTOL), Battery Systems, Charging Infrastructure
- **Location:** South Burlington, VT (onsite, 188,500 sq ft manufacturing facility); flight test center in Plattsburgh, NY
- **Timing:** Summer 2026 (active posting on Lever and Glassdoor; titled "2026-2027 BETA Internship (Vermont)")
- **Apply URL:** https://jobs.lever.co/beta/a3cf2927-7c4a-459d-8296-28034dccbee8 or https://beta.team/open-positions
- **Pay:** $22.00–$32.00/hr

**Fit analysis:** Beta Technologies is more vertically integrated than most eVTOL competitors — they design and manufacture not only the ALIA aircraft but also the charging infrastructure network (50+ sites across the US and Canada). The company is targeting FAA type certification for the CX300 (conventional takeoff variant) by end of 2026, with the VTOL A250 to follow. Their South Burlington manufacturing facility is built on a 40-acre site, entirely powered by solar and geothermal energy, and designed for up to 300 aircraft/year with expansion capacity. The internship program places interns across multiple manufacturing tracks: Composites, Battery, Motor, Final Assembly Line (FAL), Structures, Subsystems, and Machine Shop. For a PhD materials science student, the strongest angle is the Composites or Battery track, where materials qualification and process development are active concerns as Beta pushes toward certification. Beta also received a $368M funding round led by Fidelity (backed by Amazon's Climate Pledge Fund), a $20M New York State investment for Plattsburgh expansion, and has contracts to deliver electric aircraft and chargers to US Air Force bases — providing a thin but real defense-adjacent thread. The vertically integrated model means an intern could see materials flow from raw stock through layup, assembly, and charging infrastructure integration in a single company, which is unusual.

**Potential gaps:** Beta's internship listing is a general application — you apply and are placed on a team based on skills and company needs. There is no guarantee of landing on a composites or battery track versus being placed in machine shop or supply chain. South Burlington, VT is a real relocation with limited housing options; Burlington is a small city. The $22–$32/hr pay range is lower than Joby's graduate student rate. Beta is less well-known than Joby or Archer, which means less brand recognition on a resume but potentially more responsibility and access as an intern. The CX300 is a conventional takeoff aircraft (not VTOL), which is less technically exotic than competitors' tilt-rotor or multirotor designs — the composites work may be more similar to conventional aircraft manufacturing than to novel eVTOL challenges.

## Pivotal (formerly Opener) — Internship, Manufacturing Engineering (Summer 2026)
- **Score estimate:** 62/100
- **Sector:** Advanced Air Mobility — Ultralight eVTOL (Part 103), Consumer Personal Flight
- **Location:** Palo Alto, CA (onsite)
- **Timing:** Summer 2026 (active postings on Glassdoor and Pivotal careers page for multiple engineering internship tracks)
- **Apply URL:** https://www.pivotal.aero/careers (Summer 2026 internships in Manufacturing Engineering, Mechanical Engineering, Flight Test, GNC, Software, Electrical)
- **Pay:** ~$25.00/hr

**Fit analysis:** Pivotal (formerly Opener, backed by Larry Page) builds the Helix, an ultralight single-seat eVTOL that flies under FAA Part 103 regulations — no pilot's license required, starting at $190,000. The company claims to be on track to deliver its first customer Helix in Q1 2026, which would make it the first eVTOL company to reach consumer delivery. The Manufacturing Engineering Intern role involves supporting prototype fabrication, assembly, and testing — this is a smaller-scale, higher-iteration-rate manufacturing environment compared to Joby or Archer. For a PhD materials science student, the potential value is in the speed of learning: Pivotal is a smaller company where an intern may touch more of the product and process than at a larger eVTOL company. The Helix uses composite airframe construction, and as Pivotal scales from prototype to production, materials and process consistency become critical. However, the role description emphasizes CAD (CATIA, Solidworks) and mechanical design support rather than materials testing or qualification — the materials science hook is thinner here than at Joby or Archer. The Palo Alto location is expensive but central to the Bay Area aerospace ecosystem.

**Potential gaps:** Pivotal is a smaller, more secretive company (Larry Page-backed, historically low-profile). The intern program is less formally structured than Joby or Archer's cohort-based programs. The ultralight Part 103 category means less rigorous FAA certification requirements than Part 23 type-certificated aircraft — so the "certification-driven materials qualification" narrative is weaker here. There is no confirmed materials-specific intern track; the Manufacturing Engineering Intern and Mechanical Engineering Intern roles are the closest fit, and neither explicitly calls for materials science background. This is cold-outreach territory for a materials-specific pitch — apply to the Manufacturing Engineering Intern posting but include a cover letter explaining what materials depth brings to their production scale-up. Pay at ~$25/hr is the lowest of the four companies listed here, and Palo Alto cost of living is among the highest in the country. No confirmed intern housing support.

## Priority Recommendation

**Tier 1 — Apply if pursuing eVTOL composites manufacturing experience:**
1. **Joby Aviation** — Strongest composites manufacturing fit. Furthest along toward FAA certification. Graduate student pay is competitive. The Marina facility is a real production floor, not a lab. Apply to the Composite Manufacturing Internship via iCIMS.
2. **Archer Aviation** — The M&P Engineering Intern role is the most materials-science-aligned posting in the eVTOL sector. CFRP testing, adhesive bonding, materials qualification for FAA certification — this is the language of a materials PhD. Confirm the posting is still live on Greenhouse before investing time in an application.

**Tier 2 — Worth applying with realistic expectations:**
3. **Beta Technologies** — General application with team placement. Angle for Composites or Battery track. More vertically integrated than competitors, which means broader exposure. Air Force contracts provide a minor defense-adjacent signal. Lower pay and Burlington relocation are real considerations.

**Tier 3 — Cold outreach / situational:**
4. **Pivotal** — Smallest company, thinnest materials-science fit in the posted roles. But fastest iteration speed and first-to-consumer delivery. Worth applying to the Manufacturing Engineering Intern posting with a materials-focused cover letter if you want Bay Area exposure and a smaller-team environment. Do not prioritize over defense-energetics or Tier 1 eVTOL roles.

**General eVTOL sector notes for a PhD materials science candidate:**
- Security clearance is irrelevant in commercial eVTOL. Do not lead with it.
- The strongest hooks are: (1) composites manufacturing process knowledge, (2) materials qualification and testing for certification, (3) battery thermal management and failure analysis, (4) data-driven manufacturing optimization (computational background).
- eVTOL should be positioned as a secondary sector — the defense-energetics roles (RTX, Northrop, L3Harris, PSI) are higher-alignment opportunities where clearance and national lab experience are hard differentiators. eVTOL is a backup that provides legitimate composites floor experience without the defense premium.
