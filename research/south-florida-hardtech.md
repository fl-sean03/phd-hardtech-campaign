# South Florida Hardtech Startups — PhD Materials Science Summer 2026

*Research conducted March 2026. Covers the Miami-Dade, Broward, and Palm Beach county corridor (Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton, Deerfield Beach, Hollywood, Margate, Riviera Beach, Jupiter, West Palm Beach, Delray Beach). Focus: small hardtech startups (<200 people) with startup pace/energy that could fit a materials science PhD intern.*

*This region is NOT a traditional hardtech corridor like Austin, the Bay Area, or Denver/Boulder. The ecosystem is real but early-stage and thin. Most opportunities here require cold outreach rather than formal ATS applications. The strongest fits are in additive manufacturing, battery/energy materials, and composites. Defense tech is emerging but driven more by conferences and ecosystem-building (eMerge Americas) than by a deep base of SBIR-funded startups.*

---

## Ecosystem Overview

South Florida's hardtech ecosystem is nascent but growing. Key dynamics:

- **eMerge Americas** is the anchor event, pivoting hard toward national security/defense tech. The 2nd NATSEC Demo Day (April 21, 2026 at PortMiami) will feature dual-use startups demoing for DoD buyers. The full conference (April 22-24, 2026) covers autonomous systems, energy security, space, and venture funding. Attending or volunteering could be a high-signal networking play.
- **Hard Tech Miami** is a community of 100+ builders (founded by Channing Pear, ex-Stripe, and Alexandra Pear) connecting founders, VCs, and engineers. Members span IoT, robotics, drones, biotech, manufacturing, solar, defense. Regular roundtables, dinners, pitch workshops. This is the grassroots network to plug into.
- **Moonlighter FabLab** (Miami Beach) is a nonprofit makerspace/fabrication lab with 3D printers, laser cutters, CNC, vacuum forming. Partners with Hard Tech Miami and hosts Maker Faire Miami. Good prototyping resource.
- **35Mules Innovation Hub** (FPL/NextEra) is an accelerator for clean energy and energy-adjacent startups. Alumni network ("The Herd") provides ongoing support. Based in Miami.
- **Defense VC activity** is growing nationally ($49.1B in defense tech VC in 2025, up from $27.2B in 2024), and Miami is positioning itself to capture some of this flow, but the actual company density is still low compared to DC/NoVA, Austin, or LA.
- **Palm Beach County** has $10.7B in government defense contracts but this is dominated by large primes (Pratt & Whitney, L3Harris/Aerojet Rocketdyne, Lockheed Martin/Terran Orbital), not startups.

Sources: [eMerge Americas National Security](https://emergeamericas.com/national-security/), [Hard Tech Miami](https://www.hardtechmiami.com/), [Moonlighter FabLab](https://moonlighterfablab.org/), [35Mules](https://www.35mules.com/), [Refresh Miami hardtech scene](https://refreshmiami.com/news/miamis-hard-tech-scene-is-bigger-than-you-might-think-and-growing/), [South Florida + Defense](https://emergeamericas.com/south-florida-defense/)

## Honest Assessment

**The bottom line:** South Florida is NOT a top-tier destination for a materials science PhD hardtech internship. The ecosystem has 2-4 genuinely excellent opportunities (ATI AM in Margate, Sintavia in Hollywood, MATERIAL in Miami) and then a long tail of moderate-to-poor fits. Compare this to the Denver/Boulder corridor (Ursa Major, Solid Power, Sierra Space, Electra, Forge Nano, Quantinuum — all within 30 min) or the Bay Area (50+ relevant companies).

**If you're going to South Florida for personal reasons** (family, lifestyle, specific interest), the strategy is:
1. Apply formally to ATI Additive Manufacturing Intern (the one confirmed posting)
2. Cold outreach Sintavia and MATERIAL (the two best startup fits)
3. Use Pratt & Whitney / Aerojet Rocketdyne as corporate fallbacks
4. Network through Hard Tech Miami and eMerge Americas

**If location is flexible**, the Colorado opportunities in the master list are objectively stronger and require no relocation from CU Boulder.
