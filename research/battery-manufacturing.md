## Application Framing for Battery Manufacturing Sector

**For solid-state / ceramic electrolyte companies (QuantumScape, Factorial):**
Lead with ceramic interface physics: "My PhD research on interface failure mechanisms under cycling is the same physics that determines whether your ceramic electrolyte resists dendrite penetration or fractures — I model computationally what your characterization team measures experimentally. I want to close that loop."

**For silicon anode companies (Enovix, Amprius, Group14):**
Lead with mechanics of cycling-induced failure: "Silicon expands 300% during lithiation. I model how interfaces survive — or fail — under that kind of cyclic mechanical loading. Your anode architecture is a manufacturing solution to the physics problem I study. I want to see how processing parameters on the production floor map to the failure modes I simulate."

**For cathode/materials processing companies (6K, Mitra Chem, Forge Nano):**
Lead with processing-structure-property: "I use DFT and molecular dynamics to predict how processing conditions control material properties at the atomic scale. Your manufacturing challenge — tuning plasma parameters, or ALD coating thickness, or synthesis conditions to hit electrochemical performance targets — is the industrial version of my thesis. I want to connect my computational predictions to your production data."

**For manufacturing-heavy companies (Form Energy, Group14 at scale):**
Lead with translation experience: "I've worked in the DOE ecosystem translating computational results into guidance that non-computational engineers can act on. I want to spend a summer on the manufacturing floor seeing how that guidance connects to real production decisions."

**For defense-adjacent companies (Amprius, Lyten, 6K Additive):**
Add the clearance signal: "I hold an active security clearance from prior national lab work. I can contribute to ITAR-controlled programs without onboarding delays."
