# Critical Minerals & Rare Earth Processing — Research

*Research current as of March 2026. Verify URLs and deadlines before applying.*

*Focus: Domestic critical minerals supply chain companies — rare earth mining/separation, battery recycling, permanent magnet manufacturing, and lithium extraction — for summer 2026 internships. These companies sit at the intersection of materials science, national security, and DOE industrial policy.*

These companies are assessed against a PhD materials science profile with computational simulation experience (LAMMPS/VASP/QE), HPC background, active security clearance, prior national lab/DOE ecosystem work, and interest in hydrometallurgical processing and separation chemistry. The critical minerals sector carries a strong national security dimension — the security clearance and DOE familiarity are differentiators that most applicants to these companies cannot match.

## Competitive Positioning in Critical Minerals

The critical minerals sector has a unique competitive dynamic for PhD candidates: the national security framing means that U.S. citizenship and security clearance are not just nice-to-haves but genuine differentiators. Many of these companies hold DoD contracts, operate under ITAR-adjacent conditions, or receive Pentagon-backed funding. A PhD candidate with an active security clearance who can reference prior national lab or DOE program experience is sending a signal that most applicants — including many strong MS/PhD chemists and engineers — cannot match.

The hydrometallurgical processing angle is the strongest technical hook across this sector. Redwood Materials, Ascend Elements, MP Materials (separation), and Ucore all use hydrometallurgical or solvent extraction processes as core technology. A PhD candidate who can frame their computational background as relevant to modeling separation chemistry (extraction equilibria, speciation in aqueous-organic systems, crystallization kinetics) has a technical narrative that connects across multiple companies in this space.

For the magnet manufacturing companies (MP Materials, USA Rare Earth, Noveon, Niron), the technical hook shifts to sintered magnet microstructure: grain boundary engineering, Nd-rich phase distribution, coercivity mechanisms, and the relationship between sintering process parameters and magnetic performance. A computational materials scientist who can model NdFeB phase equilibria or magnetic domain behavior has a narrative that is directly relevant and unusual among intern applicants.

The DOE ecosystem familiarity is a cultural differentiator. Every company on this list except Piedmont Lithium has received direct DOE, ARPA-E, or DoD funding. Referencing DOE technology transition programs, ARPA-E commercialization pathways, or national lab collaboration experience in cover letters will read as insider knowledge to hiring managers who have navigated those same programs.
